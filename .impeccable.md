## Design Context

### Users
Dhruva (co-founder) and hackathon demo audience — judges, investors, peers. Viewed on a laptop screen or projected. The operator submits a task and the audience watches the AI make a real phone call in real time. Context: high-attention, live demo, often in a darkened room.

### Brand Personality
Live. Operational. Inevitable.
Not "cool AI tool" — more like mission control for your phone agent. The transcript streaming in IS the product. Everything else is chrome.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Mission Control** — dark with warm amber accent. Feels like a NASA ops room or algorithmic trading terminal. Flat ruled lines not card borders. Condensed technical labels. The live transcript stream is the hero element. Status changes feel urgent. Something is happening RIGHT NOW.

Anti-references: generic AI dashboards (cyan-on-dark, glowing cards), SaaS admin UIs, generic dark-mode with blue accents.

### Fonts
- Display/labels: Chakra Petch — technical HUD readout feel, not in the default AI font pool
- Body/transcript: Figtree — clean grotesque, reads well at small sizes in transcript format

### Colors (OKLCH)
- Background: oklch(11% 0.01 60) — near-black, warm amber tint
- Surface: oklch(16% 0.012 60)
- Surface elevated: oklch(21% 0.015 60)
- Accent: oklch(72% 0.18 55) — amber/orange, "something is happening"
- Text primary: oklch(92% 0.008 60)
- Text secondary: oklch(60% 0.012 60)

### Design Principles
1. The transcript stream IS the product — give it space and visual weight
2. Status is felt, not just read — amber pulse for live calls signals urgency
3. Flat ruled lines, not cards — mission control uses grids, not tiles
4. Everything inline — no modals, no overlays, no hidden states
5. Empty states teach — the input placeholder shows an example call
